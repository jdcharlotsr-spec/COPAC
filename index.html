To be Apostolic means we are built upon the foundation of the early church described in the Book of Acts. Sacred Beliefs

The Godhead

We believe in the absolute Oneness of God—the one true and living God, who was manifested in the flesh as Jesus Christ. He is the Father in creation, the Son in redemption, and the Holy Ghost in emanation.

Isaiah 9:6, Colossians 2:9

Water Baptism

Baptism is more than a ceremony; it is a burial. We baptize by total immersion in the Name of the Lord Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, following the pattern of the New Testament Church.

Acts 2:38, Romans 6:4

Salvation

The full New Testament experience: Repentance from sin, Water Baptism in Jesus' Name, and the Infilling of the Holy Ghost with the initial evidence of speaking in tongues.

John 3:5, Acts 19:1-6

Christian Liberty

While firm in our Apostolic beliefs, we are non-sectarian. We fellowship ecumenically with all who proclaim Jesus as the only saving Name, honoring the diversity of the Body of Christ.

Galatians 5:1, Acts 4:12

Communion

We offer both wine and grape juice. Communion is observed at least four times throughout the year specifically when our monthly gathering falls on a First Sunday.

1 Corinthians 11:23-26

Prayer & Intercession

We believe in fervent, effectual intercession. Prayer is our primary weapon of warfare and our direct line of communion. We believe that prayer shifts atmospheres and changes lives.

James 5:16, 2 Chronicles 7:14

Headcoverings

Following 1 Corinthians 11 as a sign of spiritual order, we acknowledge the biblical significance of this practice. However, we leave the observation to the personal conviction of the individual believer.

1 Corinthians 11:1-16

Holy Living

A heart-felt desire to reflect the character of Christ. We are called to be set apart in our conduct, our dress, and our spirits for His glory.

Hebrews 12:14, 1 Peter 1:16
